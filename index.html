About the Hackathon

The Climate Action Hackathon 2025: Urban Heat & Flood Resilience is a thematic innovation event focused on tackling the growing climate challenges in urban areas — especially rising temperatures and frequent flash floods.

This hackathon brings together students, developers, designers, and community innovators to co-create digital solutions that support SDG 13: Climate Action. Participants will work in teams to build prototypes, apps, or visualization tools that help communities:

  • Mitigate urban heat through green infrastructure mapping, smart shading, or cooling strategies.
  • Improve flood resilience via early-warning systems, response tools, or interactive dashboards.
  • Raise climate risk awareness using data visualization, storytelling, or geospatial tools.

Workshops, mentoring sessions, and data resources will be provided — empowering participants to transform ideas into impactful, community-driven digital products that promote environmental responsibility.

How Solutions Reduce Climate Risks

Two approaches for SDG 13: mitigating climate change causes and adapting to its unavoidable impacts.

Mitigation of Climate Risk

Mitigation actions focus on reducing the greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions that drive climate change. Key solutions include:

  • Transition to renewable energy sources like wind and solar.
  • Improving energy efficiency in industries, buildings, and transport.
  • Promoting sustainable land use and afforestation.
  • Investing in clean technologies and carbon capture solutions.

Adaptation and Resilience Building

Adaptation measures strengthen the capacity of systems and communities to handle climate impacts. Key solutions include:

  • Strengthening disaster risk reduction and early warning systems.
  • Integrating climate strategies into local and national policies.
  • Improving awareness and capacity for impact reduction.
  • Mobilizing climate finance to support vulnerable communities.
